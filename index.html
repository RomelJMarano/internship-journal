Emelyn S. Ulanday

Pre-Service Teacher · Bachelor of Elementary Education

This Teaching Internship Portfolio is a reflection of my journey as a pre-service teacher, showcasing the knowledge, skills, and experiences I have gained throughout my internship at Malobago Elementary School. Each entry is a testament to my evolving pedagogical approach, the challenges I've overcome, and the milestones I've achieved. It represents not only the lessons I have taught but also the lessons I have learned—about students, teaching, and myself as an educator.

Throughout this internship, I have had the opportunity to apply educational theories in a real classroom setting, develop meaningful relationships with students, and collaborate with experienced mentors. This portfolio highlights my growth in lesson planning, classroom management, instructional strategies, and assessment practices. It also includes reflections, sample lesson plans, student work, diverse activities I conducted and feedback that demonstrate my commitment to continuous learning through creating a supportive and effective learning environment.

As you explore this portfolio, I hope it conveys the passion, dedication, and continuous effort I bring to the teaching profession. I hope you find inspiration in my journey and gain a deeper appreciation for the noble profession of teaching. I welcome you to the world of endless learning and growth. As you delve into the pages, let me share to you the beginning of my lifelong journey as an educator—one built on growth, reflection, and a deep desire to make a positive impact in the lives of students.

Planning a lesson is one thing — planning a curriculum is another. I learned to think beyond the single lesson and consider where students are coming from and where they need to go. Section 9

My Teaching Philosophy as a Beginner

As a Beginning Teacher

"

I believe that every child comes to school with a story — a unique background, a distinct way of seeing the world, and an inherent capacity to grow. As a beginning teacher, my role is not simply to deliver content, but to create the conditions in which every child feels seen, valued, and capable.

Teaching, for me, begins with relationships. Before a student can truly learn from me, they must first trust me. I am committed to building that trust through consistency, genuine care, and the conviction that I believe in every learner's potential — even when they do not yet believe in it themselves.

I approach every lesson with the question: How will this help my students grow — not just as learners, but as people? I believe in active, meaningful learning — learning that connects to students' lives, that challenges them to think critically, and that empowers them to apply knowledge beyond the walls of the classroom.

I also believe that great teachers are lifelong learners. My time as a pre-service teacher at Malobago Elementary School Site 2 from January 19 to March 27, 2026 taught me more than any textbook could. I learned that flexibility, empathy, and reflective practice are as essential as any pedagogical skill. I am still learning — and I always will be.

As I begin my professional journey, I carry with me this conviction: Education is the most powerful act of hope we can offer to a child. I am honored to be a part of that work.

— Emelyn S. Ulanday, Beginning Teacher
